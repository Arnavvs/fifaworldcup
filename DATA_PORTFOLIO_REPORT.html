3. Honest Gaps & Blockers

Stale FIFA rankings
Our FIFA rank features cut off at 2024-04. For a tournament starting June 2026, this is a meaningful staleness risk. Fix effort: Easy — scrape the published monthly ranking PDFs or tables.
Year-end ELO vs per-match ELO
ELO is joined as the most recent year-end value. A team that rises or falls sharply within a year will look mis-rated. Fix effort: Medium — source the full per-match ELO series from eloratings.net.
FBref & Transfermarkt blocked by anti-bot
We have complete scrapers ready, but they are Cloudflare-blocked from this machine. Running from a residential IP or using Playwright/Selenium would populate: xG, possession, SCA, player minutes, tackles, injuries, and club-season form. Fix effort: Medium–Hard — requires browser automation stack.
No recent betting odds (2016–2026)
The market is the single strongest predictive baseline. Without it, we cannot calibrate probabilities or detect value. Fix effort: Medium — OddsPortal via Playwright is the best free path.
Team-name string joins
Cross-source joins rely on normalized names (e.g., “USA” vs “United States”). We have built dim_team (575 canonical IDs), but some downstream tables still use raw strings. A one-time normalization pass is the highest-leverage schema fix. Fix effort: Easy
AI Input — Risk Prioritization
From a project-management standpoint, I would rank these by expected log-loss improvement per hour of work:
1. Live FIFA rankings (huge lift, 2–4 hours)
2. Team-name normalization (prevents silent join failures, 2–3 hours)
3. Recent odds (enables calibration & value betting, 1–2 days)
4. Per-match ELO (sharpens strength signal, 1 day)
5. FBref/Transfermarkt unblocking (richest data, 2–3 days but requires infra)
Do not try to unblock everything at once. Ship a model with #1–#4 first, then invest in #5.

AI Input — Model Strategy
Start with LightGBM + CatBoost as a stacked ensemble. Use Dixon-Coles as the simulation engine, not the classifier — it respects football’s low-score dynamics better than any ML model. Do not invest in deep learning (FT-Transformer, GNN) until you close the xG + odds + lineup gaps. Right now, the data is too thin on high-resolution signals for deep models to outperform gradient boosting. Save the GPU budget for later.
